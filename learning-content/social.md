# Social Engineering & Email Fraud

**Category:** Linguistic Audit

**Read time:** 5 mins

**Description:** Identify emotional manipulation, urgency coercion, fake authority hooks, and security warnings scammers use to trigger submissive panic.

![Social engineering placeholder](images/social.png)

## Content

Social engineering leverages cognitive biases: scarcity (limited-time offers), authority (impersonated executives or agencies), and urgency (deadlines) to short-circuit critical thinking. Verify sender domain and headers, avoid replying with sensitive information, and treat unexpected requests for money or credentials as high risk. Train to ask: "Is this request verifiable via official channels?" and to escalate to security teams before acting on financial or credential requests.
