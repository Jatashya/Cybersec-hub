## Content

Phishing domains often rely on visual similarity (homograph attacks), typosquatting, and subdomain tricks (e.g., paypal.example-login.com). Inspect the full hostname (from the right-most registered domain) rather than only the left-most labels. Check certificate details by clicking the padlock, avoid following shortened links without previewing, and use offline heuristics like the Aegis analyzer to flag raw IPs, suspicious TLDs, and high-entropy hostnames. When uncertain, open the site in an isolated environment or use a dedicated link scanner.
