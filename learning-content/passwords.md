# Securing Passwords & Entropy

**Category:** CRYPTOGRAPHY

**Read time:** 4 mins

**Description:** Learn how bits of entropy dictate password safety, why character length beats complexity, and how to protect against dictionary and offline cracking rigs.

## Content

Cryptographic security relies on entropy. Short passwords with symbol substitution (e.g. "P@ssw0rd1!") are still vulnerable to modern hybrid dictionary attacks and GPU-accelerated cracking. Prefer long passphrases (20+ characters) that are easy to remember but have high search-space size; for example, "correct horse battery staple" is stronger than short complex strings. Use a reputable password manager, unique passwords per service, and enable 2FA (TOTP or hardware keys) wherever possible. Regularly audit exposed credentials using breach notification services and rotate passwords on high-value accounts.
